Few info about Cleveland Cavaliers

The Cleveland Cavaliers are a professional basketball team based in Cleveland, Ohio, competing in the Eastern Conference of the National Basketball Association (NBA). The franchise was established in 1970, and its history has been marked by both highs and lows. The Cavaliers gained widespread recognition in 2003 when they selected LeBron James as the first overall pick in the NBA Draft. LeBron's tenure with the team, particularly from 2003 to 2010 and then again from 2014 to 2018, propelled the Cavaliers to multiple playoff appearances and their historic NBA Championship victory in 2016. Despite facing challenges in the post-LeBron era, the Cavaliers continue to be a resilient and evolving team, focusing on developing young talent and building a competitive roster for the future.
