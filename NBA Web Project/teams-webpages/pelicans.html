Few info about New Orleans Pelicans

The New Orleans Pelicans, established in 2002 as the New Orleans Hornets, later underwent a significant rebranding in 2013 to become the Pelicans, reflecting a connection to the region's rich cultural and natural heritage. The franchise relocated to New Orleans from Charlotte, North Carolina, where they were known as the Hornets. The early years in New Orleans were marked by both on-court struggles and moments of promise. In the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, the team temporarily played home games in various cities before returning to New Orleans in 2007. Despite challenges, the Hornets became a resilient and integral part of the New Orleans community.

The rebranding to the Pelicans coincided with a new era for the franchise. The team drafted Anthony Davis as the first overall pick in the 2012 NBA Draft, and he quickly became a cornerstone player. The Pelicans, with Davis leading the way, enjoyed some success in the Western Conference, making playoff appearances and advancing to the second round. However, Davis eventually sought a trade, leading to his departure from the team. The Pelicans, undeterred, pivoted toward a new era of building around emerging star Zion Williamson, the first overall pick in the 2019 NBA Draft. The young and talented roster, combined with the team's commitment to community engagement, has positioned the Pelicans as a team with potential for future success and a strong connection to the unique culture of New Orleans.
