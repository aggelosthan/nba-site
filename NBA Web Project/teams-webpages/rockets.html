Few info about Houston Rockets

Established in 1967 as an expansion team, the Houston Rockets have emerged as a dynamic force in the National Basketball Association (NBA). The team was founded by Robert Breitbard and was originally based in San Diego before moving to Houston in 1971. Early in their history, the Rockets faced challenges but began to find success in the 1980s under the leadership of iconic players like Hakeem Olajuwon. Known as "The Dream," Olajuwon became the face of the franchise and led the Rockets to back-to-back NBA championships in the 1993-1994 and 1994-1995 seasons, securing their place in NBA history. The team's commitment to excellence during this era solidified the Rockets as a powerhouse, and Olajuwon remains one of the greatest centers in the history of the sport.

The Rockets continued to be competitive in subsequent years, with notable players like Tracy McGrady and Yao Ming making significant contributions. In recent times, the team underwent a transformative period, marked by the acquisition of James Harden, a prolific scorer and playmaker. Under Harden's leadership, the Rockets consistently made deep playoff runs, coming close to securing an NBA championship. Despite falling short, the Rockets have remained a formidable presence in the NBA landscape, continually striving for success and representing the city of Houston with pride. The team's rich history, marked by both triumphs and challenges, showcases the resilience and determination that characterize the Houston Rockets as a respected franchise in the world of professional basketball.
