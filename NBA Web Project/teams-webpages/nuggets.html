Few info about Denver Nuggets

The Denver Nuggets have a compelling history in the NBA that dates back to their establishment in 1967 as an expansion team. The franchise originally began as the Denver Larks but quickly changed their name to the Denver Rockets. In 1974, the team rebranded once again, becoming the Denver Nuggets. Throughout the 1970s, the Nuggets experienced success in the American Basketball Association (ABA) under legendary coach Larry Brown. Players like David Thompson and Dan Issel became synonymous with the team during this era. The Nuggets transitioned to the NBA in 1976 following the ABA-NBA merger, and their high-scoring style of play continued to define their identity.

One of the most iconic moments in Nuggets history occurred during the 1984-85 season when the team engaged in a classic playoff series against the Los Angeles Lakers. The series, known as the "Miracle at Richfield," featured memorable performances by Alex English and Kiki Vandeweghe but ultimately ended in a heartbreaking loss for the Nuggets in a decisive Game 5. Despite this setback, the Nuggets have remained a competitive force in the NBA, with notable periods of success, including the late 2000s and recent years. The emergence of stars like Nikola Jokic and Jamal Murray has brought the team back into the playoff spotlight, making the Denver Nuggets a team to watch in the Western Conference.
